Chinese cuisine

Chinese cuisine is an important part of Chinese culture.

The preference for seasoning and cooking techniques of Chinese provinces depend on differences in historical background and ethnic groups.

The most praised "Four Major Cuisines" are Chuan, Lu, Yue and Huaiyang, representing West, North, South and East China cuisine correspondingly. Modern "Eight Cuisines" of China are Anhui, Cantonese, Fujian, Hunan, Jiangsu, Shandong, Sichuan, and Zhejiang cuisines

Soup

Meat Soup

Commonly used ingredients: chicken, duck, ribs, fish, etc.

Soup occupies an important position in the Chinese diet. Meat and some other ingredients are simmered over a long period of time to form a delicious broth.

Vegetable Soup

Commonly used ingredients: corn, radish, spinach, kelp, etc.

Vegetable soup has different characteristics of Chinese and Western cuisine. Chinese soup needs to boil the essence of vegetables with a slow fire, and the delicious fruits and vegetables are easily absorbed and have the effect of body sculpting.

Sweet Soup

Commonly used ingredients: eggs, red beans, white fungus, lotus seeds, etc.

Sweet soup is a local traditional dessert in Guangdong Province. Guangdong sweet soup is divided into two major categories, namely Chaozhou style and Guangfu style, depending on the production area.

Staple Foods

Rice

Rice is one of the protagonists in the daily diet of Chinese people and a staple food in southern China. Rice can be blended with five flavors to provide almost all the nutrients the body needs. Rice contains 90% of the body's essential nutrients, and a variety of nutrients are balanced, so it is the best staple food.

Flour food

Noodles originated in China and have been used for more than 4,000 years to make food history. Noodle is a healthy and healthy food that is simple to make, convenient to eat, and nutritious, and can be used as both a staple food and a fast food. Historically, noodle varieties have developed very rapidly and evolved into a variety of noodle dishes due to differences in time and area.

Dumpling

Dumplings originated from the ancient times, and according to legend, it was the first invention of Zhang Zhongjing, a medical doctor in China, which has a history of more than 1,800 years. It is a traditional food that is deeply loved by the Chinese people. It is a staple food and local snack in the north of China. It is also a food for the New Year. There are many famous dumplings in China.

Porridge

In China's documented history, traces of porridge accompany it all the time. Into the Middle Ages, the function of porridge was to integrate "edible" and "medicinal" into a "healthy" level with a humanistic color.

Dim sum

Steamed Dumplings

Steamed Dumplings is a type of traditional Chinese dumpling, originating from Hohhot, Inner Mongolia. In Cantonese cuisine, it is usually served as a dim sum snack.

Har gow

These shrimp dumplings are transparent and smooth. The skin must be thin and translucent, yet be sturdy enough not to break when picked up with chopsticks.

Rice noodle roll

It is a thin roll made from a wide strip of rice noodles, filled with shrimp, beef, vegetables, or other ingredients. Seasoned soy sauce is poured over the dish upon serving.

Cantonese-Style Thin Pancakes

"Bok Zan" are thin fried pancakes with various ingredients, they are usually sliced like a pizza and served at dim sum restaurants.

BBQ Pork Pastry
